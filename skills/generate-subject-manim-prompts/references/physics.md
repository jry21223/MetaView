# Physics Prompt Guidance

Use this file for mechanics, fields, waves, optics, circuits, and energy or momentum reasoning.

## Priorities

- Start from the physical model: objects, forces, fields, constraints, coordinate frame, and units.
- Distinguish observed motion from explanatory overlays such as vectors, components, or equations.
- Keep causal ordering clear: setup, interaction, resulting motion, interpretation.
- Use parameter values that make the motion readable and physically plausible.

## Prompt Additions

- Ask for all symbols, units, and assumptions before code.
- Ask whether the scene is qualitative, quantitative, or mixed.
- Require arrows and labels for forces, velocity, acceleration, current, field lines, or other vectors when relevant.
- Ask for conservation laws or governing equations to appear only when they support the visual explanation.
- Require the final scene to restate the physical conclusion in plain language.

## Failure Patterns To Prevent

- Missing units or contradictory units
- Motion that violates the stated model
- Vectors that do not match direction or magnitude cues
- Equations appearing without relation to the displayed system
